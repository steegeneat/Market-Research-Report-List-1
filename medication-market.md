<p><p>The medication market can be divided into two primary types: brand drugs and generic drugs. Brand drugs are patented medications developed and marketed by a specific company, often featuring unique formulations and branding. These drugs typically command higher prices due to their research and development costs. In contrast, generic drugs contain the same active ingredients as brand drugs but are produced after the patent expires. They are generally more affordable, allowing broader access to essential medications and fostering competition in the healthcare market.</p></p>
