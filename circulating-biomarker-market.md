<p><p>The circulating biomarker market comprises various types, primarily including circulating DNA, circulating tumor cells (CTCs), and other biomarkers. Circulating DNA involves fragments of genetic material released into the bloodstream, often utilized for cancer detection and monitoring. CTCs are intact cells shed from tumors into circulation, providing insights into cancer progression and treatment response. Other markers may include proteins, exosomes, or metabolites that offer additional diagnostic or prognostic information. Each type plays a crucial role in precision medicine and personalized therapy.</p></p>
