<p><p>The mobile phone battery market comprises mainly two types: Lithium Ion (Li-ion) and Nickel Metal Hydride (NiMH) batteries. Li-ion batteries dominate due to their high energy density, longer lifespan, and lightweight, making them ideal for modern smartphones. They support fast charging and have lower self-discharge rates. In contrast, NiMH batteries, while offering better performance in low-temperature environments, are heavier and less efficient than Li-ion, leading to their decline in popularity for mobile applications.</p></p>
