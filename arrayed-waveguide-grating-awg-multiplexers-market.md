<p><p>Arrayed Waveguide Grating (AWG) multiplexers are key components in optical communication systems, distinguished primarily by their thermal and athermal types. Thermal AWGs rely on temperature changes to tune their optical performance, making them more sensitive to environmental conditions but often more cost-effective. In contrast, athermal AWGs maintain stable performance across varying temperatures, offering superior reliability for critical applications but typically at a higher cost. Both types serve to efficiently manage signal multiplexing in fiber optic networks.</p></p>
